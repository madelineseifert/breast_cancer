BREAST CANCER RESEARCH FUNDING


Cancer research has led to significant advances in cancer prevention, detection, diagnosis, and treatment.

Non-profits and research universities are leading the oncology research funding efforts across the United States. Below is an overview of secured funding for breast cancer research by State.





California is the state with the largest research budget (funding) in the United States with ~$640 million over the span of 2009-2013. Leading the way was UCSF accounting for nearly ~$150 million. The state diagnoses nearly 135,000 people annually with one of the four cancer types with largest incidence: breast, prostate, lung, and colorectal cancer. Of those, nearly 30% succumb to the disease.

Massachusetts (Brigham & Women's Hospital), New York (Columbia U), Pennsylvania (UPenn), and Texas (U of Texas) follow as the next leading research funding states in the country.
