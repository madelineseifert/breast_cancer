About our Model

Only four user inputs from the largest tumor nucleus are required for our tumor diagnosis model. These inputs can be prepared by imaging a microscope slide containing fluid drawn via FNA from a breast tumor. One software tool that is recommended for analysis is ImageJ.

Variable Information
Radius
Concave Points
Area
Perimeter
  • The radius of an individual nucleus is measured by averaging the length of the radial line segments de fined by the centroid of the nucleus.
  • Determine the center of the largest tumor nucleus and draw radial segments from there.
  • In order to capture shape information we measure the number of concavities or indentations in a cell nucleus.
  • Draw chords around the largest tumor nucleus and if any chords exist outside of the nucleus, it is a concave point.
  • Nuclear area is measured simply by counting the number of pixels on the interior of the largest nucleus.
  • This can be done by drawing a rough outline of the tumor nucleus and calculating the number of pixels within.
  • The total distance of the largest nuclear perimeter.
  • By drawing chords around the nucleus you get this measurement.
